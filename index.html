The Student Journey

From first line of code
to flight day.

01 / Code

Day one:
your first line of flight code.

Walk in with no programming background. By the end of week one you've written Python that takes a Tello off the ground, runs a square pattern, and lands. We teach by flying — every concept maps to a real maneuver in the air.

02 / Build

Week six:
solder your own airframe.

Move from off-the-shelf kits to custom builds. Brushless motors, ESCs, flight controllers, battery harnesses — assembled at our workbench. You flash the firmware, tune the PIDs, and the aircraft you fly is the one you built.

03 / Launch

Graduation:
from code to career.

Top students move into our operations division — aerial mapping, infrastructure inspection, commercial flight contracts. Others go on to university UAV programs or industry roles. The classroom is the start, not the finish.

Who We Teach

Built for
every level.

Audience Kids & Teens — after-school clubs

Kids & Teens — after-school clubs

Ages 9–16. Two-hour sessions twice a week. Scratch-based programming, indoor flight, and end-of-term mission challenges. No prior coding required.

University students — UAV electives

Semester-length tracks for engineering and computer-science majors. Coursework in flight dynamics, embedded systems, and autonomy that complements existing university curricula.

Professionals — industry training

Short, intensive certifications for engineers, surveyors, and operators moving into UAV work. Includes flight-hour logging and operator licensing prep.

Schools & institutions — partnerships

On-site programs delivered at schools and technical institutes across Uzbekistan. We bring the drones, the curriculum, and the instructors.
