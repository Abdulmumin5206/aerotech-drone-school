The Aerotech
advantage

Real flight time, not simulators.

Every program includes structured flight hours on DJI Tello EDU and custom-built airframes. Students log indoor manoeuvres, GPS missions, and obstacle courses from week one. We measure progress in airtime and lines of code shipped — not lectures attended.

Curriculum built by working engineers.

Modules are designed alongside Tashkent-based UAV operators and university faculty. The same flight controllers, ROS stacks, and Python libraries used in industry are what students learn here.

A workshop, not a classroom.

Soldering stations, 3D printers, parts bins, and an indoor flight cage. Students disassemble, repair, and modify aircraft as part of the standard track.

From hobby to career path.

Graduates move into university UAV programs, internships with our operations partners, and certified pilot tracks. We hire from our own roster.

Basics

Code your
first flight.

Eight-week introductory track where students program a DJI Tello EDU in Python and Scratch. By week three, every cohort runs autonomous indoor missions. By week eight, choreographed flight in formation.

Read program details
Build

Solder your
own airframe.

A twelve-week build track where students assemble a custom quadcopter from parts: brushless motors, ESCs, a flight controller, a battery harness. They flash firmware, tune PIDs, and fly what they made.

Read program details
Advanced

Mission-grade
autonomy.

For students who finished Basics or arrive with prior experience. PX4, ROS 2, computer-vision pipelines for object tracking, and waypoint missions over real terrain. Fieldwork happens at our outdoor flight site.

Read program details
Operations

Fly for
a living.

A professional certificate track preparing graduates for commercial work — aerial mapping, infrastructure inspection, agricultural surveys, and last-mile delivery. Launching alongside our operations division.

Who We Teach

Built for
every level.

Kids & Teens — after-school clubs

Ages 9–16. Two-hour sessions twice a week. Scratch-based programming, indoor flight, and end-of-term mission challenges. No prior coding required.

University students — UAV electives

Semester-length tracks for engineering and computer-science majors. Coursework in flight dynamics, embedded systems, and autonomy that complements existing university curricula.

Professionals — industry training

Short, intensive certifications for engineers, surveyors, and operators moving into UAV work. Includes flight-hour logging and operator licensing prep.

Schools & institutions — partnerships

On-site programs delivered at schools and technical institutes across Uzbekistan. We bring the drones, the curriculum, and the instructors.
